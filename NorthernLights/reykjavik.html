Reykjavik

Reykjavik is made of extreme contrasts. Widely known as “The Land of Fire and Ice” Iceland is home to some of the largest glaciers in Europe, and some of the world's most active volcanoes. Iceland is also the land of light and darkness. Long summer days with near 24-hours of sunshine are offset by short winter days with only few hours of daylight.

Vik's Beaches
In 1991, the American journal Islands Magazine counted the black sand beach of Vik, Iceland as one of the ten most beautiful non-tropical beaches on Earth. It’s easy to see why! Vik is Iceland’s southernmost village and faces the open Atlantic Ocean, framed by a long black volcanic sand beach.

Skogafoss

 

Skogafoss (or more accurately Skógafoss; "SKOH-ga-foss") probably rivals Gullfoss as Iceland's most famous waterfall. This was a classically-shaped rectangular waterfall that dropped 60m with a width of 25m, and it was this feature that prompted Julie to call this her favorite waterfall in the country. Adding to its allure was that it had a high volume of water making it thunder and produce rainbow-yielding mist that often would make this falls a photographer's dream under sunny skies.

Golden Circle Tour
The ‘Golden Circle’ constists of a visit to the incredible Gulfoss waterfall, the impressive Geysir with bubbling hot water that spouts from the ground and the stunning National Park at Þingvellir. On this trip you will visit all of these locations with addition of the chance to explore one the underground hidden gems in the form of a lava tube on a caving trip. This is a great chance to visit the three main attractions closest to Reykjavik with the added benefit of the chance to see what is below ground.

Caves in Iceland
Explore the hidden side of Iceland with a visit to a lava tube in the Blue Mountains just outside of Reykjavik. Discover the unusual rock formations and a chance to experience complete darkness as we walk and crawl in this stunning hidden underground environment.

Glacier Tours
Have you ever wanted to walk on a glacier? Well now is your chance! On the Blue Ice tour we visit the stunning Sólheimajökull glacier in the south of the country. Our expert guides will talk to you about walking on a glacier, get you kitted out with an axe and crampons and take you up onto the ice. You will discover what it is like to walk on this 500 year old ice and learn about the formation and structure. There is an option to try ice climbing too, which is a fascinating and rewarding experience. On the way back to Reykjavik we will visit the stunning Skógafoss waterfall.

Experience the best of Reykjavik

Hunting the Northen Lights Hunting the northern lights from the ocean is an amazing experience; sailing away from the coastline of Reykjavik and seeing the aurora borealis out on the North-Atlantic ocean with beautiful mountains and the city lights in the background is something you won't forget. During the tour our expert guide will tell you all about the northern lights, the folklore and superstition, as well as explaining the underlying scientific facts that make this magical light show possible. The tour takes 2 – 3 hours, departing from Reykjavík´s old harbour; viewing the beautiful coastline of the capital and the city lights on the way out to search for the lights. In only 15-30 minutes we will be on a prime location for viewing the auroras. Included are warm overalls since it does get chilly in the winter and usually much of the time is spent outside gazing at the night sky.
